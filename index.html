RAD MUTUAL EDUCATION

ABOUT US

Who We Are

Rad Mutual Education is an organization committed to bringing STEM (science, technology,engineering, & mathematics) education to our communities through organized skillshare and incollaboration with mutual aid organizations.Individually, we are computer science students at Western Washington University who wish tosee our communities thrive, and believe that providing STEM education for the youth is one ofthe most effective ways of doing that.

What We Provide

Exposure to various fields is beneficial to personal growth and understanding - no matter whatyour student hopes to study someday, learning about computer science is expanding theirknowledge of what’s out there.

Why It Matters

We provide students within and in association with our communities with free STEM education.At this time, we are only offering free regular computer science and programming lessons forstudents ages ten and up via Zoom. Your student does ​not​ need to know computer science,programming, or math. Each unit we teach will introduce a wide range of important computerscience topics and skills.
If you do not have computer or internet access, we will help coordinate with local resources,such as schools, libraries, and community members, to provide suitable technologies. We willprovide free snacks, books, and paper activity drop-offs to ensure that we are clearing as manyobstacles as possible for kids who would like to learn with us..

Where Community Thrives

We will continue to grow RME to meet the needs of our community, with immediate efforts toeliminate traditional barriers to learning by collaborating with mutual aid groups. Weencourage you to connect with us and share thoughts on how to best support the educationalneeds of our community as this change occurs.
